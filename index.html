Phase 01 - FoundationSlide 1A
What is AI,
Really?
The model, not the myth
🧠
AI is pattern recognition at scale
It does not think. It finds the most statistically likely next word, image, or action based on billions of examples it trained on. That is all it does.
📑
It learned from human-written text
Books, articles, websites, code, conversations. GPT-4 trained on roughly 1 trillion words. It absorbed human knowledge - and human bias - at once.
⚠
It has no memory, no opinion, no intent
Every response starts fresh. It is not hiding things from you. It is not trying to manipulate you. It is completing a pattern. That is its entire existence.
Trainer note
Ask the room: "Put your hand up if you think AI is intelligent." Then: "Keep it up if you think a calculator is intelligent." Watch the room recalibrate.

Phase 01 - FoundationSlide 1C
The 5-Part Prompt Formula
Every great AI output starts with a great input
1
Role
"You are an expert HR manager at a UAE university..." — give the AI a persona. It changes the vocabulary, tone and depth of every response.
2
Task
"Write a 200-word welcome email for a new student joining our MBA programme..." — be specific. Vague task = vague output. Every time.
3
Context
"The student is from Nigeria, starting in September, and this is their first time studying outside their home country..." — context is the difference between generic and useful.
4
Format
"Use a warm, professional tone. No bullet points. End with a call to action to attend orientation..." — tell it how to structure and present the output.
5
Constraint
"Do not mention tuition fees or payment. Keep under 200 words." — tell it what NOT to do. Often more powerful than what you tell it to do.
Live example — put it together
Full prompt
[Role] You are an experienced HR manager at a UK-validated university in the UAE.

[Task] Write a welcome email for a new student joining the MBA programme.

[Context] The student is from Nigeria, starting September 2025. First time studying outside their country.

[Format] Warm and professional. No bullet points. End with a call to action to attend orientation.

[Constraint] Under 180 words. Do not mention tuition or payment.

What is a Workflow?
Making AI do the repetitive work so you can do the meaningful work
▶
Trigger
Something happens that starts the process. A form is submitted. An email arrives. A date is reached. A payment is made. The trigger is the "when".
⚙
Action
The system does something in response. Sends an email. Creates a record. Generates a document. Updates a spreadsheet. The action is the "what".
🧠
AI Step
The AI adds intelligence. It reads, writes, and decides. Student enquiry arrives, AI reads it, writes a personalised response, email sent. Zero human involvement.

Phase 04 - Responsible AI - Slide 4A
Hallucination,
Bias, Copyright
The real risks - not the science fiction ones
💥
Hallucination
AI makes things up. Confidently. Plausibly. With citations that do not exist. It is not lying - it is pattern-completing without knowing what it does not know. Always verify facts, dates, names, and numbers.
⚖
Bias
AI learned from human-written text. Human text contains bias. It will reflect cultural, gender, and geographic bias from its training data. In the MENA context, Western-centric defaults are especially common.
©
Copyright
AI-generated content does not have clear copyright protection in the UAE or UK. Do not submit AI output as original work without disclosure. Do not paste copyrighted material into AI tools without understanding the terms.
Live hallucination detector - press L to launch poll
Ask ChatGPT a question about LUBM history or a local UAE fact. Participants independently verify one claim. Vote: True / False / Cannot verify. Watch how many cannot verify a confident AI statement.
The 3-check rule
1
Before you trust it
Would this be embarrassing if wrong? Verify before using in any official document, student communication, or public content.
2
Before you share it
Run a quick Google search on any specific fact, date, name, or statistic the AI gives you. Takes 30 seconds.
3
Before you submit it
Has the AI made any claims you cannot independently verify? If yes, remove them or add a caveat.

Phase 04 - Responsible AI - Slide 4B
LUBM AI Policy
Framework
What responsible AI use looks like at this institution
DO
use AI to draft, summarise, and structure
Use AI as your first draft. You edit, verify, and approve. You are the author. AI is the typist.
DO
disclose AI use where required
Student assignments, research outputs, and external publications should note where AI was used.
DO
keep sensitive data out of public AI tools
Student records, financial data, and personal information must not be pasted into ChatGPT or Claude.ai.
DO
verify before publishing
Any AI output that goes to students, partners, or the public must be reviewed by a human.
DO NOT
NOT use AI to make decisions about individuals
Admissions, grades, HR decisions - AI can inform but humans must decide and be accountable.
UAE AI Context
UAE National AI Strategy 2031
The UAE is one of the world's most AI-forward governments. The strategy targets AI contribution of 35% of GDP. Every public institution is expected to embed AI. LUBM is ahead of the curve by training staff today.
York Validated Programmes
York St John University has issued guidance on AI in academic work. Students must disclose AI use. Staff using AI in course design should retain records. When in doubt, disclose.
Your personal rule
"Would I be comfortable if my line manager could see exactly how I used AI to produce this?" If yes, proceed. If not, reconsider.
